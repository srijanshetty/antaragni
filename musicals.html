Antaragni Idol, the most popular of the musical competitions of Antaragni, is taking a giant leap this time. Having dealt with some of the extremely talented singers, now it is ready to put all the young voices of India to test. Come and gauge your talent with some of the gifted children of mother India.

Have you ever thought why the singer always stands at the centre? Do you know that when a common man listens to a song, it is to the vocals that he is paying most of his attention to? Do you know how much a band with excellent musicians but a bad singer is going to suffer? Yes, the saying truly says – “Singer is the king of orchestra” There is certainly some magic in human voice which isn’t there in any other sound. Every human voice has got an unmatchable flavour which makes it unique. Having witnessed breath-taking western solo performances in the past years, Antaragni is now waiting to hear your unique voice. Any western genre is allowed. So if you have ever considered upgrading from the bathroom singer to a pop singer – or, for that matter, to be the next Jackson – Aria invites you to sing your favourite piece.

Rules & regulations:
Rules and Guidelines:
1.Maximum Participation: 3 participants from a single college/institute.
2.There will be only one round in this competition. The participant has to sing a song of his/her own choice.
3.Time Limit: 5 minutes (including sound check).
4.A maximum of 2 instruments and 2 accompanists can be used for accompaniment. The performer may play an instrument while singing.
5.Acoustic Guitar and Synthesizer will be provided.
6.Any western genre is allowed.
7.A major portion of the song’s lyrics should be in English.
8.The decision of the Judges and the coordinators shall be final and binding in all cases.
Judgment criteria:
The judgment would be based solely on the vocal abilities of the performer. Accompaniments, whether played by the performer or by other members, will not be given any credit.

1st Prize: Rs. 3000
2nd Prize: Rs. 1500

Ever got inspired by Yanni, Pandit Hariprasad Chaurasia, Andy McKee or Zakir Hussain? Want to put your swift arms and skillful fingers to display before an encouraging and enthusiastic audience? Here’s the opportunity for you. Come and entertain the music lovers who are eager to listen to your performance. This year, Antaragni promises to present you with some of the best musicians you probably would have ever heard to.

Rules & regulations:
1.Maximum Participation per college: No constraint (subject to availability of seats)
2.There will be only one round in this competition.
3.More than one piece is allowed within given time limit.
4.Time Limit: 8 minutes (including sound check)
5.Instruments provided: Tabla, Harmonium, Acoustic Guitar, Electric Guitar, Synthesizer, Drum kit. The participant is supposed to bring any extra instruments, if needed. The maximum number of accompanists allowed is 2 (including the lead performer).
6.At no point of time throughout the performance a person shall play more than one instrument.
7.No one shall sing.
8.Accompaniments with respect to Taal and Sur (Lehra with percussion instruments and Taal with Tune instruments) are compulsory for classical performances.
9.A flutist can use multiple flutes, keyboardist can change patches, and percussionist can use multiple percussion instruments within the same performance if he/she wishes to.
10.Guitar processors, Samplers, Midi Programming, Arpeggios, Loops from computer or any other electronic device or any software related sound processing is strictly not allowed.
11.The decision of the Judges and the coordinators shall be final and binding in all cases.
Judgment criteria:
1.Judgment will be based on the ability of the lead performer alone. Other musicians will be given no credit.
2.Judgment will be based on the ABILITY of the lead performer, rather than how huge or wide the sound is.

1st Prize: Rs. 4000

A simple competition with simple rules and one of the most liked and attended events of all the Musical competitions of Antaragni – Pair on Stage calls on stage a couple, and gives them the freedom to perform whatever they wish to!! Any combination of instruments or vocals or both is allowed.

Rules & regulations:
Team size 1.Maximum Participation: 3 duos from a single college/institute
2.There is only one round in the competition where the couple has to give a performance of their own choice.
3.Time Limit: 6 minutes (including sound check)
4.Instruments provided: 1 Drum Kit, 1 Synthesizer, 1 Acoustic Guitar, 1 Tabla pair. The participants are required to bring any instrument they need other than those mentioned above. No bar on the number of instruments!
5.No extra accompanists or karaoke will be allowed.
6.Same member singing and playing an instrument is allowed.
7.Any member changing instruments in-between would not be given any extra time, and any such time will be included in the performance time.
8.The decision of the Judges and the coordinators shall be final and binding in all cases.
Judgment criteria:
1.Coordination between the team members
2.Individual mastery of the piece, with equal emphasis on both the performers in a team, shall be given due consideration.
3.Level of difficulty of the composition and overall performance

Human Voice undoubtedly sounds better than any other musical instrument. Saying that, what about an artistic blend of such voices? Ears are getting eager right? Love the Harmonies of Bohemian Rhapsody? Love to sing Indian folk songs together with a group? Ever dreamt of being part of a choir? Sangam is for you.

Rules & regulations:
1.Participation: A maximum of 2 teams per college having no common member.
2.Team size: Minimum 6 and maximum 12 vocalists. Maximum 3 accompanists.
3.A team member can sing and play an instrument as well. However such a person would be counted as both vocalist and accompanist.
4.There will be only one round.
5.Time limit: 10 minutes (including sound check)
6.Instruments provided: Harmonium, Acoustic Guitar, Drum Kit, Synthesizer and a Tabla pair will be provided. The team is supposed to bring any other instruments if needed. However, not more than three instruments can be played at any point of time.
7.The team may sing more than one song within the time limit.
8.Language: Any Indian Language and English as well
9.The decision of the Judges and the coordinators shall be final and binding in all cases.
Judgment criteria:
1.Coordination among team members
2.Emphasis would be more on vocals and less on accompaniments.
3.Overall performance
4.Stage Presence, Audience appeal

Love melody? Want a break from the harsh, distorted and synthetic sounds? Hungry for smooth and soothing Acoustics? Have a bite of Unplugged. The adorable Piano sound, mesmerizing Strings, and the soft wind instruments are ready to blow your mind. Jazz or Ghazal, Latin or Qawwali, Folk or Fusion; any Raag, any Rhythm; our stage awaits your performance. What are you waiting for?

Rules & regulations:
1.Participants: Minimum 5 and maximum 10 members in the band and maximum 2 bands from a single college/institute. There should not be any common band member.
2.There will be only one round in this competition.
3.Time Limit: 15 minutes (including sound check)
4.Instruments provided: Drum kit, Acoustic Guitar, Synthesizer, Bass Guitar, Harmonium, Tabla. The performers are supposed to bring any other acoustic instruments (like flute, sitar or dholak), if needed.
5.Synthesizer can be used in place of piano and in absence of Acoustic Bass Guitar, Electronic Bass (or even Bass from synthesizer) can be used.
6.No electric instruments of any other kind are allowed.
7.Other electronic devices like Guitar/ Vocal processors, Samplers, Midi programming, Arpeggios, Loops from computer or any other electronic device or any software related sound processors are strictly not allowed.
8.Participants will not be provided with sustain/damper pedals. They are expected to bring any such additional accessories they require.
9.Language – English, Hindi or a mixture of both.
10.Genres like Dub step, Rock, Grunge or Growl are strictly not allowed. Indian pure Classical pieces are not allowed but semi-classical songs are allowed.
11.Original Composition includes – song composition, musical arrangement and song writing.
12.The decision of the Judges and the coordinators shall be final and binding in all cases.
Judgment criteria:
1.Level of difficulty, vocal/instrumental ability, tonal quality, coordination between team members and overall performance.
2.Improvisation, ingenuity will be given extra credit. However, Original Compositions altogether will carry substantially higher weightage.
3.Originality – Innovation in the interpretation of works, presenting self-compositions.
4.Stage presence, Audience appeal.

1st Prize: Rs. 12000
2nd Prize: Rs. 6000
3rd Prize: Rs. 3000

Rules & regulations:
1. Team size: min 5 and max 12
The event consists of 2 rounds:
ROUND 1: ELIMINATION
1. Participant teams get to perform their prepared street dance sequence.
2. Time limit: 6-10 minutes(length of music track).
3. Live music is not allowed.
4. Props are allowed.
5. Deviating from the dance style can lead to negative marking.
6. Hindi Bollywood music is strongly discouraged, it can lead to negative marking.
7. The dance floor will be a preferably rectangular one in an open arena.

ROUND 2: FACE-OFF

1. Out of all participants, 4 teams will qualify for this round and will play 2 matches per team in total.
2. This round comprises of teams taking part in street/hip-hop battle against randomly chosen opponents.
3. Each battle would be of 6 minutes with the two teams performing for 30 seconds alternately.
4. At least 1 team member must occupy the stage during the performance of respective teams.
5. It is strictly notified that the face-off is a hip-hop battle and deviation from hip-hop might result in negative marking.
6. Division of a team’s performance time amongst all team members is expected and will be included in the judging criteria.

Judging criteria:

* Following is the weightage to be given to both rounds of the event:-
Round 1:- 50%
Round 2:- 50%

*Judging criteria includes synchronization, choreography, stage utilization, costumes, energy level, prop usage, stage coverage, innovation, presentation, and overall impact on the crowd.
(For FACE-OFF, crowd support and most of all for overshadowing opposite team.)
**In case of conflicts, decision of the judges would be considered final.

1st Prize: Rs. 10000
2nd Prize: Rs. 5000
3rd Prize: Rs. 3000
