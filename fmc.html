Cronica is a short film-making competition, the ultimate platform that each artist deserves. Amaze your viewers with your perspective, aesthetic sense, emotion or simple ingenuity of idea. So ignite the director, cinematographer, the sound specialist, the editor or simply an art connoisseur inside you waiting to leap out.

Theme

The theme is open but your film should fall under any one of the following genres strictly: 1. Thriller 2. Social Issue

Rules & Regulations:
1. The film should be between 10 to 12 minutes including credits.

2. Entries should have been made after July’14.

3. Each production team will have one to five registered members. Only one entry per group will be permitted.

4. Any type and number of video or film camera or cameras may be used for filming. Animation and computer work is also permitted, and you may combine techniques as you deem fit. The teams will not be provided with any equipment or computers for editing or any other purpose.

5. The content of the film must be appropriate for the public screening. Filmmakers should assume at most a PG13 mentality when scripting projects. The organizers reserve the right to not screen any film that deem inappropriate for the public event.

6. If shot in 2.35:1, and/or any type of widescreen format, your film must be letter-boxed for 4:3 or 16:9 ratio. The projector will not be switched to accommodate other ratios. If we cannot view your film on our equipment, it may be disqualified.

7. Effective portrayal of theme, Editing, Cinematography, Script, Acting, Sound design and overall impact is used for judging criteria.

8. Films do NOT have to be in English/Hindi, although if you choose to make a film in another language, it must contain English sub-titles.

9. Entries must be the original work of the Entrant; must not infringe third-party's rights; must be suitable for public (i.e., may not be obscene or indecent); must not contain obscene or pornographic material.

10. Entries must be made in accordance with the competition instructions. If they are submitted any later than the specified closing time, they will be declared invalid.

Submission:
Completed movies (accompanied by the name of the college and details of team members along with their role in the making of the movie) must be submitted before 11:59, 5th of October2014 in .mpeg or .avi formats only (though .mpeg format is known to provide better quality videos than .avi) in one of the following ways:

1. Emailing to fmc.antaragni14@gmail.com
Note that the size of the file may be too large to email depending on your video clip, and your email provider. In such a case, use one of the other methods.
2. Uploading the video on www.youtube.com or any other such website and mailing the link to fmc.antaragni14@gmail.com Your should have resolution of atleast 480p. Your Youtube video should have a description about the story in the ‘description tab’ provided on the website.

The folk dance competition truly depicts the varied culture and traditions of India. From Gujarat's Garba to Assam's Bihu dance, and from Punjab's Bhangra to Goa's Ghode Modni, the folk dance competition, Roots has been and will continue to add colours to the festival.

Rules & regulations:
1. Teams have to perform one of the following dance forms: Alkap, Bhangra, Bhavai, Bihu, Cheraw (bamboo dance) , Chhau, Dhap, Dumhal, Gambhira,Garba (dandiya), Gaur dance, Ghoomar, Ghumura, Giddha, Gondhal, Jawara,Jhoomer, Kalbelia, Karakattam, Koli, Lavani, Naga dance, Odissi, Padhar, Raas.
2. If team wants to perform a form other than those mentioned above, they are required to contact the coordinators well in advance.
3. Time Limit: 5-10 minutes.
4. Team size: minimum 6, maximum 12.
5. Costumes and props would be included in the judging criteria.
Judging Criteria:
Choreography, authenticity (costumes, props, stylistic elements, music etc.), energy, stage coverage, synchronization and overall impact.
**In case of conflicts, decision of the judges would be considered final.

1st Prize: Rs. 8000

A call out to all the shutterbugs out there! *APERTURE* is the online photography competition for Antaragni ’14. We give you a platform to give the world a peek through your eyes. Prizes worth 40k at stake so start clicking.

REGISTRATION:

2. The photographs should be in JPEG format only.
3. Please provide information on the following along with your entry:

• Name
• Phone number
• College
• Camera Used
• Caption
RULES:

1. Four different themes will be provided for different weeks starting from Sept 7th, 2014.
2. Size of the pictures should not be less than 1280*960 pixels; this is close to 2.0 mega pixels (for digital photographs).
3. Composition, lighting, technique and presentation are the main components of the competition.
4. You can use any type of camera and digital camera but make sure it is presentable.
5. Editing Photographs in any kind of software is not allowed (cropping is allowed).
6. Each entry must be the original creation of the entrant and should not contain any third party content which is not the property of the contestant.
7. Antaragni reserves the right to disqualify entries with offensive or inappropriate content or those which promote any specific brand, at any point in the contest.
8. The copyright of the pictures will stay with the entrant but Antaragni reserves the right to use the pictures for their publicity and other activities.
9. One one entry per participant per theme is allowed.
SELECTION AND JUDGING:

- Facebook response (depending on the number of likes) (30%).

- Judge’s score (70%), which will be based on the following:

1. Technical quality

4. Relevance to theme

Rules & regulations:
1. Team size: min 5 and max 12
The event consists of 2 rounds:
ROUND 1: ELIMINATION
1. Participant teams get to perform their prepared street dance sequence.
2. Time limit: 6-10 minutes(length of music track).
3. Live music is not allowed.
4. Props are allowed.
5. Deviating from the dance style can lead to negative marking.
6. Hindi Bollywood music is strongly discouraged, it can lead to negative marking.
7. The dance floor will be a preferably rectangular one in an open arena.

ROUND 2: FACE-OFF

1. Out of all participants, 4 teams will qualify for this round and will play 2 matches per team in total.
2. This round comprises of teams taking part in street/hip-hop battle against randomly chosen opponents.
3. Each battle would be of 6 minutes with the two teams performing for 30 seconds alternately.
4. At least 1 team member must occupy the stage during the performance of respective teams.
5. It is strictly notified that the face-off is a hip-hop battle and deviation from hip-hop might result in negative marking.
6. Division of a team’s performance time amongst all team members is expected and will be included in the judging criteria.

Judging criteria:

* Following is the weightage to be given to both rounds of the event:-
Round 1:- 50%
Round 2:- 50%

*Judging criteria includes synchronization, choreography, stage utilization, costumes, energy level, prop usage, stage coverage, innovation, presentation, and overall impact on the crowd.
(For FACE-OFF, crowd support and most of all for overshadowing opposite team.)
**In case of conflicts, decision of the judges would be considered final.

1st Prize: Rs. 10000
2nd Prize: Rs. 5000
3rd Prize: Rs. 3000
